MedPrompt: LLM-CNN Fusion with Weight Routing for Medical Image Segmentation and Classification

Authors: Shadman Sobhan, Kazi Abrar Mahmud, Abduz Zami, Mohiuddin Ahmed, Md Palash Uddin

Journal: Biomedical Signal Processing and Control Q1 IF 4.9

Medical image analysis often demands the simultaneous handling of multiple tasks — segmentation and classification — across diverse imaging modalities. This paper introduces MedPrompt, a unified framework that synergizes a few-shot prompted Large Language Model for high-level task planning with a modular Convolutional Neural Network (DeepFusionLab) for low-level image processing.

The core innovation lies in a dynamic weight routing mechanism: the LLM interprets a natural language prompt to select and configure the appropriate CNN pathway, enabling a single model to flexibly perform segmentation or classification without retraining. This fusion of symbolic reasoning and deep feature extraction achieves strong performance across multiple benchmark datasets, demonstrating that bridging language and vision models can yield clinically practical, adaptable AI systems.

UltraLightCPS: An Ultra-Lightweight Colon Polyp Segmentation Model using Adaptive Compressed Multi-Scale Semantic Fusion Mechanism

Authors: Abduz Zami, Shadman Sobhan, Mohiuddin Ahmed, Md Palash Uddin

Journal: Biomedical Signal Processing and Control Q1 IF 4.9

Early and accurate detection of colon polyps is critical in preventing colorectal cancer, yet deploying Deep learning models in clinical environments remain challenging due to computational constraints. This paper introduces UltraLightCPS, a novel segmentation architecture built around an Adaptive Compressed Multi-Scale Semantic Fusion mechanism, designed to capture rich multi-scale contextual features while operating at extreme parameter efficiency — achieving competitive accuracy with only 0.23M parameters.

By compressing and fusing semantic information across multiple receptive fields adaptively, UltraLightCPS maintains high segmentation fidelity on standard polyp benchmarks while remaining deployable on resource-constrained hardware. This work advances the frontier of lightweight medical image segmentation, making AI-assisted colonoscopy analysis more accessible in low-resource healthcare settings.

A Multi-Stage Deep Learning Approach to Tuberculosis Detection with Explainable Insights

Authors: Shadman Sobhan, Abduz Zami, Mohiuddin Ahmed, Tanvir Mahtab Zihan, Tanvir Ahmed Khan, Aranya Saha

Conference: NCIM 2025 (Dhaka)  |  Publisher: IEEE Xplore

Tuberculosis (TB) remains one of the leading infectious disease killers globally, and reliable automated detection from chest X-rays could significantly aid resource-limited health systems. This work presents a multi-stage pipeline combining segmentation for precise lung region segmentation with a downstream classification model, ensuring that the classifier focuses on diagnostically relevant anatomy.

A central contribution is the integration of Explainable AI (XAI) techniques — specifically SHAP and Grad-CAM visualizations — that highlight which regions of the X-ray most influenced the model's prediction. This transparency is essential for clinical adoption, allowing radiologists to audit and trust the model's decision-making process rather than treating it as a black box.

Skin Cancer Classification Using Pre-trained CNNs: A Transfer Learning Approach Addressing Imbalanced Data Challenges

Authors: Shadman Sobhan et al.

Conference: NCIM 2025 (Dhaka)  |  Publisher: IEEE Xplore

Skin cancer classification from dermoscopic images is complicated by severe class imbalance — benign lesions vastly outnumber malignant ones in clinical datasets — which leads to biased models that underperform on the minority classes that matter most. This paper leverages transfer learning from pre-trained CNNs (including VGG, ResNet, and EfficientNet variants) combined with advanced data augmentation strategies to effectively counter this imbalance.

Through systematic evaluation of multiple architectures, the study identifies optimal fine-tuning strategies that maximize sensitivity on rare malignant classes without sacrificing overall accuracy. The findings provide a practical recipe for practitioners adapting large-scale vision models to small, imbalanced medical datasets, a challenge common across dermatology and oncology AI.

TIG-UNet++: A Thresholded Input Guided Modified UNet++ for Separating Lung Region in Chest X-Rays

Authors: Abduz Zami, Shadman Sobhan et al.

Conference: ICTIS 2025 (Bangkok, Thailand)  |  Publisher: Springer Nature

Accurate lung segmentation from chest X-rays is a fundamental preprocessing step for many downstream diagnostic AI systems. This paper proposes TIG-UNet++, an enhanced modification of the UNet++-L4 architecture that incorporates a thresholded version of the input image as a late-stage guide, injecting structural prior information directly into the decoding pathway.

By introducing the thresholded input at a strategically chosen layer rather than at the network entry, the model preserves learned deep features while benefiting from the coarse boundary information provided by the threshold. Experiments on standard chest X-ray segmentation benchmarks demonstrate improved delineation of lung boundaries, particularly in pathological cases where opacity or consolidation obscures normal tissue contrast.

A Lightweight Framework for Facial Emotion Recognition: Leveraging Geometrical Priors with Spatial Features

Authors: Abduz Zami, Shadman Sobhan et al.

Conference: NCIM 2025 (Dhaka)

Facial emotion recognition in the wild is challenged by variations in lighting, pose, and occlusion that degrade purely appearance-based models. This paper introduces a multimodal deep learning architecture that fuses landmark-based geometric features — encoding the structural relationships between facial keypoints — with spatial pixel-level features extracted by a CNN backbone.

The geometric stream provides pose-invariant structural cues while the spatial stream captures texture and appearance details; their fusion yields a complementary representation more robust than either modality alone. The framework is deliberately lightweight, making it suitable for real-time deployment on edge devices, with competitive accuracy across standard emotion recognition benchmarks.

LLM-Assisted Question-Answering on Technical Documents Using Structured Data-Aware Retrieval Augmented Generation

Authors: Shadman Sobhan (Undergraduate Thesis)

Supervisor: Dr. Mohammad Ariful Haque  |  Status: Preprint (arXiv)

Standard Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems excel at plain-text retrieval but struggle with technical documents that contain structured data — tables, figures, equations, and mixed scanned-and-digital formats common in engineering and scientific literature. This undergraduate thesis proposes a structure-aware RAG pipeline that explicitly handles these heterogeneous document elements, extracting and indexing tables and images alongside text for holistic retrieval.

The pipeline supports both searchable and scanned PDF formats through integrated OCR, and routes queries to the most appropriate retrieval modality based on inferred question type. When paired with a large language model, the system produces accurate, grounded answers to complex technical questions that would defeat naive chunk-based RAG approaches, demonstrating strong performance on domain-specific engineering document corpora.

Prompt2SegCXR: Prompt to Segment All Organs and Diseases in Chest X-rays

Authors: Abduz Zami, Shadman Sobhan et al.

Journal: Heliyon  |  Status: Under Revision  |  Dataset on Mendeley

Chest X-ray interpretation encompasses a wide range of pathologies and anatomical structures, yet most segmentation models are trained for a single target. This work introduces Prompt2SegCXR, a lightweight prompt-driven model capable of segmenting all 23 classes — 6 organs and 17 diseases — in a single unified architecture. Crucially, the paper also contributes a novel dataset of expert-generated doodle prompts designed to guide the model toward user-specified targets.

The doodle prompt paradigm enables intuitive, interactive segmentation: clinicians can sketch a rough region of interest and the model refines it into a precise mask. This is a significant step toward human-in-the-loop radiology AI, combining the flexibility of prompt-based interaction with the breadth of multi-class disease coverage. The dataset is publicly available on Mendeley, and a preprint is available on arXiv.

DeepColonLab: Attention Guided Separable Receptive Field Block Enhanced Deeplabv3+ Model for Colon Polyp Segmentation

Authors: Abduz Zami, Shadman Sobhan et al.

Status: Under Review  |  Preprint available at preprints.org

DeepLabV3+ is a well-established semantic segmentation architecture, but its application to colon polyp segmentation is limited by its inability to model fine-grained local boundary information crucial for irregular polyp shapes. This paper introduces DeepColonLab, which enhances DeepLabV3+ with an Attention-Guided Separable Receptive Field Block that selectively focuses the network on polyp boundary regions at multiple scales.

The separable receptive field design reduces computational overhead while expanding the effective context window, and the attention guidance ensures that boundary-critical features are amplified throughout the encoding process. Evaluated on standard polyp segmentation benchmarks, DeepColonLab demonstrates improved Dice scores and boundary accuracy compared to the vanilla DeepLabV3+ baseline and competitive lightweight alternatives.
